In any case, the wavelength dependence equation n(lam) gives values for the extremes, red and violet, as 1.508 and 1.525 respectively.
Violet has a higher refractive index, meaning that it will bend at a sharper angle. This follows the album art.
The figure is also (somewhat) consistent with the album art. I used a horizontal incidence angle in my figure, but one could imagine that an oblique angle
would produce a similar effect seen on the art for "Dark Side of the Moon." Also like the album art, the light scatters both inside and outside the prism.
It is worth noting, however, that the "scattering" on the album art inside the prism is colored completely white, only appearing as a rainbow when it comes out the other side. In reality, you would see a rainbow inside as well, if albeit a little thinner.
